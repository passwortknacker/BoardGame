# Art & Audio Direction — Red Dragon

**Target:** a polished, juicy 2D card-battler on the bar set by **Hearthstone** — readable board,
tactile cards, satisfying impact feedback, and a cohesive fantasy theme. This document is the
presentation spec the Godot build is scaffolded for; current visuals/audio are placeholders.

## Visual pillars
- **Tactile cards.** Hover-lift + tilt, smooth drag-to-play, snap/return, glow on playable cards,
  flip on draw, dissolve/burn on destroy. (Motion primitives live in `game/Juice.gd`; the demo
  shows hover-lift, play-fly, floating combat text, and screenshake.)
- **Weighty impact.** Hit-stop, screenshake, particle burst + flash on damage; HP-bar drain tween;
  number pop. Boss attacks telegraph (already a rule) and play a wind-up → slam.
- **Readable board.** Boss + Anger/telegraph up top, Village + heroes mid, hand fanned in an arc at
  the bottom, market as a side drawer. Clarity over spectacle.
- **Cohesive theme.** Painterly fantasy; the Red Dragon is the visual centerpiece (idle breathing,
  anger states, level-up transformation).

## Asset pipeline & folders
```
assets/
  art/
    cards/        card frames per type (Mana/Weapon/Artifact/Support) + per-card art (1:1 ~512²)
    characters/   the 9 hero classes (portrait + board token)
    boss/         Red Dragon (idle/attack/anger/level-up), minions
    ui/           panels, buttons, bars, icons (mana, affinity, slots, anger)
    fx/           particle textures, impact sprites, glows, card-trail
    bg/           battlefield backgrounds
  audio/
    sfx/          UI/card/impact/heal/etc. (placeholders generated by tools/gen_placeholder_sfx.py)
    music/        battle/menu/victory/defeat loops (.ogg)
    default_bus_layout.tres   Master → Music / SFX  (wired in project.godot)
```
- **Cards are data** (`data/cards.json`); a card's art is looked up by name, so adding art never
  touches code. `game/cards/CardView.gd` draws a procedural frame now; swap in textures by binding
  the art band + frame StyleBox to loaded resources.
- Keep everything **atlas-friendly** and power-of-two where practical; target 1920×1080 design
  resolution (project uses `canvas_items` stretch so it scales cleanly to mobile/desktop).

## Audio
- Buses: **Master → Music, SFX** (`default_bus_layout.tres`). `game/AudioManager.gd` pools voices,
  loads `sfx/<name>.wav|ogg` by name, and no-ops with a warning if a sound is missing.
- Placeholder SFX exist for: `ui_click, card_play, hit, boss_hit, heal, victory, defeat`. Replace
  with sourced/foley audio; add music `.ogg` loops under `audio/music/`.

## Budget & sourcing (the real ship cost — decide early, per GAME_PLAN)
Art + audio is the largest line item and the moat to a *sellable* product. Options, cheapest →
priciest: curated **asset packs + shaders** for cards/UI; **contract** a card-frame set + the Red
Dragon hero piece (the one bespoke must-have); **full commission** for differentiation. Lock a
direction and a per-card art budget before the Phase-2 vertical slice expands to all 142 cards.
